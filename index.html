About Me

I'm a Computer Science PhD student at University of Southern California and a graaduate research assistant at Signal Analysis and Interpretation Lab (SAIL) , under the supervision of Professor Shrikanth Narayanan. Previously, I did my Bachelors in the ECE Departement at National Technical University of Athens where I worked with Professor Petros Maragos. Also, I was an external collaborator of the Machine Learning Lab at Univerity of Texas at Austin, where I worked with Professor Alex Dimakis .

Research Interests

My research focuses on developing theoretical and practical frameworks for privacy in machine learning, with an emphasis on multimodal and language-based systems. I aim to establish formal definitions of privacy that capture context-sensitive risks and design anonymization methods that balance identity protection with utility and fluency. By creating benchmarks, datasets, and scalable toolkits, I seek to advance principled approaches that both deepen our scientific understanding of privacy and enable deployable, real-world solutions.
