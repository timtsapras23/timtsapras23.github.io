About Me

I'm a Computer Science PhD student at University of Southern California and a graduate research assistant at Signal Analysis and Interpretation Lab (SAIL), under the supervision of Professor Shrikanth Narayanan. Previously, I did my Bachelors in the ECE Department at National Technical University of Athens where I worked with Professor Petros Maragos. Also, I was an external collaborator of the Machine Learning Lab at University of Texas at Austin, where I worked with Professor Alex Dimakis.

Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of applied and theoretical machine learning, developing systems that learn efficiently, generalize across modalities, and remain trustworthy in real-world deployment.

  • Privacy-Preserving Machine Learning: Interested in unlearning, differential privacy, and risk auditing techniques while connecting them to broader optimization and system design challenges.
  • Multimodal & Representation Learning: Building models that jointly leverage vision, language, and speech to improve grounding, reasoning, and cross-domain transfer.
  • Self-Supervised & Contrastive Learning: Designing efficient objectives and training pipelines that enable robust, data-efficient representation learning at scale.
  • Generative AI: Leveraging LLMs and diffusion models to enable context-aware reasoning and controllable multimodal generation.
